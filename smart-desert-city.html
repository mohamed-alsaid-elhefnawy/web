Smart Desert City

Project Overview

The Smart Desert City project is a revolutionary automation concept designed to engineer sustainable, self-sufficient human settlements in extreme desert environments. By deploying advanced embedded IoT controllers and smart electrical systems, this project models an optimized urban center capable of producing power and managing critical natural resources efficiently.

Key Core Systems

  • Smart Irrigation Network: Automated water distribution system controlled by custom soil humidity, thermal, and vapor sensors, reducing agricultural water loss in dry soil by up to 40%.
  • Dual-Axis Solar Tracker: Dynamic grid tracking solar arrays configured with microcontrollers to track the sun path in real-time, boosting overall PV panel yield by 35%.
  • Microgrid Energy Storage: An automated battery energy management system (BEMS) to balance load fluctuations and manage storage during peak heat periods.
  • IoT Environmental Telemetry: Wireless sensors reporting meteorological indicators (wind velocity, heat index, and relative humidity) to an central database.

Automation & Engineering Focus

Our research focuses on reliable loop closed control systems capable of operating without external inputs. We utilize robust industrial PLC architectures, embedded microcontrollers, and custom control code to prevent system failures due to high ambient dust and extreme temperatures.
