CNC Laser

Project Overview

The CNC Laser cutting and engraving project is a full hardware-software co-design of a high-precision computer numerical control system. Built entirely from scratch, the system processes raw G-code vectors and executes them using synchronous microstepping motor controllers and a powerful modulated solid-state laser.

Technical Innovation

  • Synchronous Multi-Axis Motion: Custom-tuned stepper motor controllers utilizing microstepping up to 1/32 steps, delivering motion accuracy down to 0.05 mm.
  • PWM Laser Modulation: Real-time laser intensity modulation based on linear vector speeds to prevent charring on delicate materials like acrylic and hardwood.
  • Custom Microcontroller Firmware: Low-latency motor and laser synchronization algorithms executed at hardware levels, parsing instructions directly from serial feeds.
  • Integrated Core Safety Systems: Optical flame sensors and emergency stop circuits that shut down active high-voltage feeds in under 100ms on fault detection.

Mechanical & Control Engineering

The mechanical frame uses rigid linear rail guides and dual belt drivers to eliminate backlash during rapid movements. The control box incorporates robust optoisolators to separate delicate microcontroller circuitry from high-current motor drivers and laser power rails, preventing noise and signal interference.
